Tacos

Description

Tacos are a staple Mexican dish consisting of a small, hand-sized corn or flour tortilla folded around a flavorful filling. Eaten by hand, a perfect taco balances savory meats or vegetables with bright, fresh toppings and a squeeze of lime juice.

Popular Styles

The flavor profile changes entirely depending on the core filling:

  • Al Pastor: Thinly sliced, marinated pork cooked on a vertical spit and served with fresh pineapple.
  • Carne Asada: Grilled, charred skirt steak or flank steak sliced thin for a deep, smoky flavor.
  • Barbacoa: Shredded beef, goat, or lamb slow-roasted over an open fire or steamed until ultra-tender.
  • Baja Fish: Crispy beer-battered or grilled white fish topped with cabbage slaw and creamy sauce.

Traditional vs. American Toppings

  • Street Style (Traditional): Kept minimal with finely chopped raw white onions, fresh cilantro, and a splash of spicy salsa on a soft corn tortilla.
  • Tex-Mex Style (Americanized): Loaded with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, cheddar cheese, and sour cream, often served in a crunchy, hard corn shell.

Ingredients for making crunchy Tacos

To make authentic, street-style beef tacos, you will need the following fresh ingredients.

The Shells

  • Tortillas: 8 to 12 small (street taco size) soft corn tortillas.

The Filling

  • Meat: 1.5 pounds of skirt steak, flank steak, or lean ground beef.
  • Marinade/Seasoning: 2 fresh limes (juiced), 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 teaspoon cumin, 1 teaspoon chili powder, and 1⁄2 teaspoon oregano.
  • Cooking fat: 1 tablespoon cooking oil.

The Traditional Toppings

  • Onion: 1 small white onion, finely diced.
  • Herb: 1 bunch of fresh cilantro, finely chopped.
  • Acid: 2 additional limes, cut into wedges for serving.
  • Sauce: Green (tomatillo) or red (rojo) salsa.

How to make Delicious Tacos

Quick Stats

  • Total Time: 20 minutes
  • Equipment: One skillet or cast-iron pan

Step-by-Step Execution

  1. Prep (5 Mins): Finely dice your white onion and chop the fresh cilantro. Mix them together in a small bowl to create your traditional street-style topping. Cut your limes into wedges.
  2. Cook (8 Mins): Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in your skillet over medium-high heat. Season your meat (steak or ground beef) with garlic, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Cook until fully browned and cooked through.
  3. Warm (2 Mins): Wipe the skillet clean or heat a separate dry pan over medium-high heat. Warm each corn tortilla for 30 seconds on each side until soft and pliable. (Pro-tip: Stack them in a clean towel to keep them warm).
  4. Assemble (2 Mins): Double up your tortillas (use two per taco so they do not rip). Spoon the hot meat into the center, top with the raw onion-cilantro mix, drizzle with salsa, and serve with a fresh lime wedge.
