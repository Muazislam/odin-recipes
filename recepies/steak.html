Steak

Description

A steak is a premium thick cut of high-quality meat—most commonly beef—that is quickly seared at high heat to lock in its natural juices. Known for its rich flavor and tender texture, a perfect steak features a deeply caramelized, savory outer crust and a juicy, tender center tailored to your cooking preference.

The cuts to know

The character of a steak depends entirely on which muscle cut you choose:

  • Ribeye: Highly marbled with pockets of rich fat, making it the most flavorful, juicy, and forgiving cut to cook.
  • Filet Mignon (Tenderloin): The leanest and most tender cut of beef, offering a buttery texture that practically melts in your mouth.
  • New York Strip: A steakhouse favorite with a tight grain structure and a thick fat cap on the edge, balancing deep beefy flavor with tenderness.
  • T-Bone / Porterhouse: The ultimate composite cut, featuring a New York strip on one side of a T-shaped bone and a tender filet mignon on the other.

How to Cook it

Steaks are traditionally prepared by pan-searing in a screaming-hot cast-iron skillet, grilling over open flames, or reverse-searing (slow-baking first, then searing at the end). Cooking levels are defined by internal temperatures:

  • Rare (125°F / 52°C): Cool, bright red center.
  • Medium-Rare (135°F / 57°C): Warm, rich pink center. Recommended for maximum tenderness and flavor.
  • Medium (145°F / 63°C): Hot, slightly pink center with a firmer texture.
  • Well-Done (160°F+ / 71°C+): Fully cooked through with no pink remaining.

Ingredients for making delicious steak

To cook a classic, restaurant-quality steak using the pan-searing method, you only need a few high-quality ingredients.

The Meat

  • Steak cut: 1 thick-cut steak (1.5 to 2 inches thick) such as Ribeye, New York Strip, or Filet Mignon.

The Sear & Baste

  • High-smoke point oil: 1 to 2 tablespoons of canola, avocado, or grapeseed oil (avoid butter or olive oil at the start, as they burn too quickly).
  • Butter: 2 to 3 tablespoons of unsalted, high-quality butter (added at the end for basting).

The Aromatics

  • Garlic: 3 to 4 whole cloves, lightly smashed with the side of a knife.
  • Fresh herbs: 2 to 3 sprigs of fresh rosemary or thyme.

The Seasoning

  • Salt: Coarse kosher salt or sea salt (used generously to build a crust).
  • Pepper: Freshly cracked black pepper.

How to make juicy steak

Quick Stats

  • Total Time: 40 minutes (including resting)
  • Equipment: Heavy cast-iron or stainless steel skillet

Step-by-Step Execution

  1. Prep (30 Mins): Bring the steak to room temperature. Pat it completely dry with paper towels. Season all sides heavily with coarse salt and black pepper.
  2. Sear (4 Mins): Get your pan screaming hot over high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of high-smoke point oil (like canola or avocado). Sear the steak, flipping every 1–2 minutes to build a deep crust.
  3. Baste (2 Mins): Drop the heat to medium. Toss in 2 tablespoons of butter, a few smashed garlic cloves, and fresh rosemary or thyme. Tilt the pan and continuously spoon the foaming butter over the steak.
  4. Rest (5 Mins): Pull the steak out when a thermometer reads 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare. Let it rest on a board for 5 minutes before slicing so the juices do not escape.
